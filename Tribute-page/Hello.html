Col. John Glenn

First person to orbit the Earth

Here are some of the Colonel's accomplishments:

  • 1942-Enlisted as a Navy Aviation Cadet
  • 1943-Promoted to First Lieutenant and joined Marine Corps
  • 1953-Sent to Korea to fly F-86 Sabre on combat missions
  • 1954-Became a test pilot and reported to the U.S Naval Test Pilot School in Maryland
  • 1956-59-Assigned to the Fighter Design Branch of the Navy Bureau of Aeronautics and attended the University of Maryland
  • 1957-Made the first supersonic transcontinental flight
  • 1959-Promoted to Lieutenant Colonel
  • 1959-John Glenn and 6 others join NASA as astronauts
  • 1962-Is on the crew of Friendship Flight, the first manned mission to orbit the Earth
  • 1964-Ran and lost a campaign for US Senate in Ohio
  • 1965-Becomes the VP of Corporate Relations at RC cola
  • 1970-Narrowly loses Democratic primary for the US Senate in Ohio
  • 1976-1999-John Glenn is elected to the US Senate from Ohio
  • 1998Glenn returned to space on the Space Shuttle on October 29, 1998, as a payload specialist on Space Shuttle Discovery
  • 2012-Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2016-John Glenn dies on December 8, 2016, at the OSU Wexner Medical Center at 95 years old. No cause of death was disclosed.
